Kweku is a tech enthusiast. He is interested in a career in mechatronics, specifically aerial robotics. As a research intern with Soko Aerial Robotics' Unmanned Aerial Systems Research Lab (UASRL), Kweku works on the development of fixed-wing and hybrid VTOL fixed-wing UAVs using locally sourced materials. He has contributed to the development of the Chichis 1P fixed-wing UAV, the WingHawk hybrid VTOL flying wing UAV and the Nkunyim fixed-wing UAV all developed by the team at UASRL. He is proficient in programming autopilots like Pixhawk and Matek boards. Additionally, Kweku works on repurposing salvaged laptop batteries into Li-Ion cells for powering the UAVs.

Ultimately, Kweku hopes to complete his master's degree and PhD degree, and take up a career as a researcher in a university or a private research lab. In his free time, Kweku likes swimming, bike riding, listening to music and hanging out with friends.

Worked with the UASRL team to develop the Chichis 1P UAV, the WingHawk UAV and the Nkunyim UAV. These UAVs were developed from scratch using locally sourced materials. Densed sytrofoam reinforced with rosewood is used to develop the airframes. Polyester cloth and epoxy resin is used to further reinforce the airframe to withstand impact. Li-Ion cells powered our UAVs, Matek F765 or the Pixhawk Cube autopilot boards are alternatively used to achieve autonomous flight and assisted flight (FBWA). Arduplane firmwire controls the autopilots and Mission Planner is used as our Ground Control Station (GCS).
